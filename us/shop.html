Dosing
The amount per single use. These are research reference ranges. Actual use should be guided by a qualified practitioner.
Frequency
How often it is taken. Daily means every day. 2-3x week means two or three times per week. Some compounds are cycled — used for a period, then paused.
Half-Life
How long the compound stays active in the body before half of it is gone. A longer half-life means less frequent dosing. A shorter one means the effect is more immediate but fades faster.
Onset
When you can expect to notice effects. Some compounds work within minutes. Others take weeks of consistent use before changes become measurable.
Form
The physical state of the compound. Lyophilized powder is freeze-dried. It is reconstituted with bacteriostatic water before use. This form has the longest shelf life and highest stability.
Purity
The percentage of the compound that is the actual intended molecule. Everything else is impurities. 99%+ purity is the research standard. Our COA documents this for every batch.
Storage
Where to keep it. 2-8°C means a standard fridge. -20°C means a freezer. Lyophilized powder can tolerate room temperature short-term. Once reconstituted, it must be refrigerated.
COA
Certificate of Analysis. A document from an independent laboratory confirming what is in the vial, at what purity, from a specific batch. This is the primary quality verification in research peptides.
Stacks With
Compounds that are commonly used together because they work through different pathways that complement each other. Stacking is not required — compounds work individually too.
Mechanism of Action
The biological process by which the compound works. How it interacts with receptors, cells, or signaling pathways. This is the scientific basis for the observed effects.
Contraindications
Conditions or situations where the compound should not be used, or should be used with extreme caution. Always review with a physician if any of these apply to you.
