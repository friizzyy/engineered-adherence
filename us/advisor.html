Remove the plastic cap from the peptide vial, wipe the rubber stopper with an alcohol swab, and slowly inject bacteriostatic water (BAC water) along the inside wall of the vial. Do not squirt directly onto the lyophilized powder, as this can damage the peptide. Gently swirl the vial until the powder is fully dissolved. Never shake vigorously. Most peptides use 1-2ml of BAC water per vial, which determines your concentration (e.g., 2ml into a 5mg vial = 2.5mg/ml). Store reconstituted peptides in the refrigerator and use within 30 days.

Unreconstituted (lyophilized) peptides can be stored at room temperature for short periods, but are best kept in a freezer for long-term storage (up to 12+ months). Once reconstituted with bacteriostatic water, always store in the refrigerator (2-8 degrees C / 36-46 degrees F). Keep vials upright, avoid exposure to direct sunlight, and use within 4-6 weeks of reconstitution. Never freeze reconstituted peptides, as ice crystal formation can denature the protein structure.

Research grade peptides are manufactured and sold strictly for laboratory and scientific research. They undergo purity testing (typically HPLC analysis) and are accompanied by certificates of analysis (COA). Clinical grade peptides are manufactured under GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) conditions and are intended for use in clinical trials or medical settings. The key differences are in manufacturing oversight, documentation requirements, and regulatory framework. Always verify purity via third-party COA when sourcing compounds.
